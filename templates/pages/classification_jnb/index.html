Classification_jnb

Here, we are going to predict the response to treatment in a group of breast cancer patients treated with several drugs belonging to the Taxane and Anthracycline family. Basically, these patients were divided into two groups according to their residual cancer burden: RCB 0/I as complete response (Considered as responder) and RCBII/III as partial response (considered as non-responder) in both of discovery and independent dataset. In our case, the data are unbalanced (the number of samples in one class is much larger than the number of samples in the other classes)
We will try to optimize prediction through 3 methods and evaluate them on data independent of the training data. These methods are:

  • - Default models
  • - Feature selection or optimal model complexity (omc)
  • - Optimization of the classification threshold
  • - Optimization of the algorithm hyperparameters
